Trescot Trails

I frequently ride on the Trescott trails because they are the nearest trail system to my house. When I first moved here this area was closed to the public. In the past six years with cooperation between the town of Hanover, Upper Valley Trail Alliance, and the Trescott Company, the land around the reservoirs were able to opened to the public and trails were built. At the end of last season a machine-built downhill mountain bike trail was built which has helped to drive more mountain bike traffic to the trail system.

Boston Lot

When I am looking to go for a longer ride but not drive very far from home, I like to go to Boston Lot. With over 30 miles of trails it's hard to run out of places to go, and I hardly ever have to retrace my path. The land manager is Dartmouth College, but the trails are maintained by the Upper Valley Mountain Bike Association (UVMBA). UVMBA holds a trail maintenance "dig day" once a year when its members and the public go out to maintain the trails. At 1030' the high point in the Boston Lots is Burnt Hill. That elevation makes Boston Lot a very fun place to ride just a short drive from my house.
